The Torus Pyramid: A Sacred Nexus

The Torus Pyramid, inspired by the Great Pyramid of Giza, stands as the spiritual and operational base for the New Roman Empire. Located above the Clutha River / Mata-Au in New Zealand—a river revered by the Kāi Tahu for its historical role in fishing and spiritual practices—this sacred structure harnesses magnetic fields to channel torus energy, a universal life force. The Torus Pyramid serves as a nexus for ancient wisdom and modern innovation, uniting the Egyptian, Greek, and Druid mystery schools in a celebration of divine knowledge.

The Mystery Schools

Within the Torus Pyramid, dedicated zones honor the deities and teachings of the Egyptian, Greek, and Druid traditions, creating a space for initiates to connect with the divine:

  • Cult of Aphrodite Zone: A rose garden sanctuary adorned with the 6-petal flower symbol, representing love, beauty, and harmony. Initiates perform rituals of fertility and passion, offering myrrh, roses, and seashells at a white marble altar illuminated by soft pink light.
  • Cult of Zeus Zone: A raised dais crowned with a bronze eagle, symbolizing Zeus’s sovereignty. This space is for meditation on leadership and justice, with a golden thunderbolt at the center, surrounded by storm-like lighting effects.
  • Cult of Pan Zone: A woodland grove with a stone altar carved with goat motifs, dedicated to Pan, the god of nature and the wild. Initiates honor the cycles of the earth with offerings of honey and wine, accompanied by flute music.
  • Cult of Athena Zone: A library-like chamber with an owl statue, symbolizing wisdom and strategy. Initiates study ancient texts and meditate on knowledge, with olive branches placed at the altar.
  • Cult of Christ Zone: A serene chapel with a cross of light, honoring Christ’s teachings of love and resurrection. Initiates pray and meditate on spiritual renewal, with candles and frankincense at the altar.
  • Cult of Thoth Zone: An Egyptian-inspired chamber with an ibis statue, dedicated to Thoth, the god of wisdom and writing. Initiates perform rituals of knowledge and magic, using papyrus scrolls and ankh symbols at the altar.
